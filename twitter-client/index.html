Twitter client

I have never been very happy with the twitter clients I have tried, they always seem to jump around and loose my place, especially when I click a link. All the inline images and autoplay videos trying to grasp for my attention as I scroll by drive me nuts.

I want to read twitter on my phone, and not give an app a bunch of bullshit permissions it doesn't need. Which usually means a webapp. In twitter's case I also don't want that webapp to share cookie storage with my normal browsing.

Coincidently I've been thinking a lot about browser compatibility, and client vrs. server rendering. The one thing that's still giving me pause about fully client rendered single page apps is Opera Mini. It has a lot of users, disproportionately the newly connected. It's common to ignore Opera Mini during development, so I wanted to try it out for myself, and see how some sites did.

Being unhappy with my Twitter client, it was the first thing to try. And I was happily surprised. The Twitter webapp on Opera Mini is fast and simple. No janky infinite scroll, just nice long paginated pages. And you have to click through to open media, making it a lot faster to read.

So, I have a new favorite Twitter client: Opera Mini.
